1. **World & Character Building (Artist & Director - Collaborative):**
    a. **Setting:** Define the high school – size, location (affects social dynamics & access to businessmen), general atmosphere (conservative, liberal, etc.). Artist provides visual concepts (sketches, mood boards).
    b. **Teacher (Protagonist):** Deep dive into his motivations – power, control, boredom, financial need? Backstory – was he abused? Is he charismatic? Artist provides visual representation.
    c. **Initial Victims (3-5):** Develop distinct personalities, backgrounds, and vulnerabilities for the first group of students. Consider their family lives, social standing, and what makes them susceptible to his blackmail. Artist provides visual representations.
    d. **Supporting Characters:** Flesh out key teachers, businessmen, and family members who will interact with the story.

2. **Blackmail & Initial Corruption (Director - Lead, Artist - Visual Support):**
    a. **The Blackmail:** Detail *how* he blackmails each girl. What secrets does he uncover? How does he leverage them?
    b. **First Encounters:** Describe the initial sexual encounters. Focus on power dynamics and the teacher’s control. Artist illustrates key scenes.
    c. **Mind-Breaking Begins:** Show the gradual erosion of the girls’ will and agency. Focus on emotional and psychological impact alongside the physical.
    d. **Establishing the Routine:** How often does he use them? What are the initial stages of their “breaking”?

3. **Prostitution Network (Director - Lead, Artist - Visual Support):**
    a. **Teacher Network:** How does he introduce the girls to other teachers? What are the dynamics within the teacher network?
    b. **Businessmen & Randoms:** How does he access businessmen and other clients? Is it through connections, a specific location, or word of mouth?
    c. **The "Experience":** Detail the sexual encounters with the different clients. Vary the brutality levels – some gentle, some rough, some involving whips and dildos. Artist illustrates key scenes.
    d. **Financial Gain:** How much does he earn? How is the money used?

4. **Escalation of Brutality (Director - Lead, Artist - Visual Support):**
    a. **Increasing Demand:** As the girls become known, demand increases, leading to more frequent and brutal encounters.
    b. **The 10-Men-at-a-Time Scenario:** Develop specific scenes showcasing the girls being used by multiple men for days. Focus on the physical and psychological toll.
    c. **Loss of Function:** Show the girls becoming increasingly vacant, losing their ability to think straight, and becoming reliant on the teacher.
    d. **Visual Representation of "Brokenness":** Artist focuses on depicting the girls’ physical and emotional deterioration.

5. **Replacement Cycle (Director - Lead, Artist - Visual Support):**
    a. **Identifying New Victims:** How does the teacher scout for new girls? What vulnerabilities does he look for?
    b. **The Transition:** How does he integrate new girls into the system, while the "broken" girls are still being used?
    c. **The "Discarded" Girls:** What happens to the girls who are too broken to function? Are they abandoned, hidden away, or used in other ways?

6. **Final Scene & Ending (Director - Lead, Artist - Visual Support):**
    a. **The Search Begins Again:** Detail the teacher actively looking for new victims, showing his satisfaction and anticipation.
    b. **Visual Closure:** Artist creates a final image that encapsulates the teacher’s predatory nature and the cycle of exploitation.
    c. **Ambiguity or Foreshadowing:** Does the ending hint at future escalation or a potential challenge to his power?
